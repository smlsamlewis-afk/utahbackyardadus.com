How Much Does an ADU Cost in Utah? 2026 Price Breakdown

July 20, 2026 · Utah Backyard ADUs
← Back to all guides

The honest answer is "it depends" — but that's not very useful when you're trying to plan a budget. Here are the real ranges we're seeing on Utah ADU projects in 2026, broken down by build type, plus the factors that move a project from the low end to the high end.

Cost by Build Type

Garage Conversion ADU: $50,000 – $120,000. The most affordable path, since you're working with an existing structure and skipping foundation and framing costs. Best for homeowners who already have a usable garage and want rental income without a large upfront investment.

Prefab / Modular ADU: $120,000 – $200,000. Factory-built and assembled on your lot. Faster to complete (typically 50 to 70% faster than a traditional build) and generally more cost-effective than a full custom build, while still meeting the same code standards.

Custom Stickbuilt ADU: $120,000 – $300,000. Built on-site from the ground up with full design flexibility. The highest ceiling on finishes and customization, and typically the highest long-term property value and rental appeal.

See our full ADU Pricing Guide for a detailed cost breakdown, or browse specific floor plans and starting prices.

What Actually Moves the Price

  • Size. Bigger units cost more in total, but the cost per square foot actually goes down as the unit gets larger.
  • Site prep. Sloped lots, poor soil, or difficult access add cost. Flat, accessible lots with nearby utility connections are cheaper to build on.
  • Finishes. Standard finishes are included in base pricing; upgraded flooring, custom cabinetry, and premium fixtures can add $10,000 to $40,000.
  • Permits. City permit fees in Utah typically run $3,000 to $12,000 depending on the municipality — and every city's process is a little different (see our Utah ADU law breakdown).
  • Utility connections. Water, sewer, gas, and electrical hookups vary significantly based on distance from your main home and existing infrastructure.

What Kind of Return Should You Expect?

Utah ADUs are renting for roughly $1,200 to $2,500 a month depending on size, location, and finishes — strongest in markets like Salt Lake City and Park City. Most owners recoup their full investment through rental income within 5 to 10 years, and a finished, permitted ADU can add 20 to 30% to your home's value on top of the rental income.
